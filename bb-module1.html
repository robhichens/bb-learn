Your Pay Stub

Every BB pay stub has the same structure. Let's walk through each line — what it is, what it means, and what to check.

Bright Beginnings Preschool Pay Period: May 5–18, 2025
Employee: Your Name Here
Earnings
Gross Pay $520.00 ▼
Your total earnings before anything is taken out. This is the number to check first if something looks wrong. Did you work the hours shown?
Regular Hours 40 hrs @ $13.00 ▼
Your standard hours at your normal pay rate. Check this against your timesheet.
Overtime $0.00 ▼
Any hours over 40 in a week, paid at 1.5× your regular rate.
PTO Used 0 hrs ▼
Planned, approved time off you took this period. Doesn't affect your Reliability Reward streak.
Deductions
Federal Income Tax −$42.00 ▼
Required by law. The amount depends on your W-4 filing. BB doesn't control this.
State Income Tax (VA) −$18.00 ▼
Virginia income tax. Required by law. BB doesn't control this.
Social Security −$32.24 ▼
6.2% of your gross pay. Goes toward your future Social Security retirement benefits.
Medicare −$7.54 ▼
1.45% of your gross pay. Funds the Medicare program.
Summary
Net Pay $420.22 ▼
What actually hits your account. The formula: Gross Pay − All Deductions = Net Pay.
Gross Pay − All Deductions = Net Pay
If net pay looks wrong, always start with gross pay. Did your hours get entered correctly? Then work down.

PTO vs. RTO

"Your normal weekly gross is $560. This week it's $416. You didn't think you did anything differently. What happened?"

PTO
Paid Time Off
Planned in advance. Approved by your director. You're paid your normal rate for those hours. Your Reliability Reward streak stays intact.
✓ Paid at normal rate
✓ Streak stays
✓ Plan ahead & request it
RTO
Request for Time Off (unpaid)
Unplanned or unapproved absence. You are not paid for those hours. Your gross pay will be lower this period. Your Reliability Reward streak resets.
✗ Hours not paid
✗ Streak resets
→ One unplanned day can change your check
Example

You normally work 40 hours and earn $560 gross.

You took one unplanned sick day (8 hours RTO).

Your hours this period: 32 hours

Your gross pay this period: $448 (not $560)

Difference: $112 less — not an error. That's the RTO.

For each situation below, tap whether it's PTO or RTO.

You requested Tuesday off two weeks ago. Your director approved it. You have the day off.
Your child is sick. You call out at 7am. You won't be in today.
You booked a dentist appointment and submitted a time-off request last Monday. It was approved.

Government requires this

If Something Looks Wrong

"You've checked your hours. You've checked your PTO. It still doesn't add up. Now what?"

1
Check your hours in your timekeeping system.
Log in and confirm your hours for the pay period match what you actually worked.
2
Check your PTO/RTO record.
Did you have any planned or unplanned time off? Was it coded correctly?
3
If it still doesn't add up — contact Alicia.
Come prepared with the specific difference you noticed.

Don't assume it's an error until you've checked your hours and PTO.

Don't wait — payroll questions should be raised within one pay period.

Don't ask your coworkers — they don't have access to your pay information.
